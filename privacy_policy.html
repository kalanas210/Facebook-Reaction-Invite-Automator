Privacy Policy

Last updated: December 2025

Introduction

FB Reaction Invite Automator ("we", "our", or "the Extension") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we handle information when you use our Chrome extension.

Data Collection

We do NOT collect, store, or transmit any personal data.

The extension operates entirely locally on your device. We do not:

  • Collect personal information
  • Track your browsing activity
  • Send data to external servers
  • Share information with third parties
  • Use analytics or tracking services

Local Data Storage

The extension uses Chrome's local storage API to store:

  • Settings: Your preferred delay times, limits, and configuration
  • Daily Count: Number of invites sent today (resets daily)
  • Date: Last reset date for daily counter

All this data is stored locally on your device only and is never transmitted anywhere.

Permissions

The extension requires the following permissions:

  • facebook.com access: To function on Facebook pages only
  • Storage: To save your settings locally
  • Active Tab: To interact with the current Facebook page

The extension only operates on facebook.com and does not access any other websites.

How the Extension Works

The extension:

  • Runs only when you click "Start"
  • Only interacts with Facebook's reaction modals
  • Does not access your Facebook account data
  • Does not read your messages or posts
  • Only clicks "Invite" buttons that you can see

Third-Party Services

We do not use any third-party services, analytics, or tracking tools. The extension is completely self-contained.

Data Security

Since all data is stored locally on your device using Chrome's secure storage API, your information is protected by Chrome's security measures.

Children's Privacy

Our extension is not intended for users under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect information from children.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The "Last updated" date at the top indicates when changes were made.
